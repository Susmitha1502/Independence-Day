    • BEFORE INDEPENDENCE...

    Incidents that occured before independence,


    what are the movements taken by our freedom fighters..

    1885

  • Formation of Indian National Congress.
  • Lord Randolph Churchil became Secretary of State for India.

    • 1905

    Partition of Bengal announced by Curzon.

    1906

    Mahatma Gandhi coins the term Satyagraha to characterize the Non-Violence movement in South Africa.

    1907

  • Congress split at Surat session where Congress split into two parts - Moderate and Extremists.
  • Lala Lajpat Rai and Ajit Sigh were deported to Mandalay following riots in the colony of Punjab.

    • 1909

    Morley-Minto Reforms of the Indian Council Act 1909 announced.

    1911

    Capital of India was shifted from Calcutta to Delhi.

    1913

    Ghadar Party formed at San Francisco to organise a rebellion in India to overthrow the British rule.

    1914

    WORLD WAR-1 started.

    1916

  • Gandhiji formed Sabarmati Ashram at Ahmedabad.
  • Foundation of Banaras Hindu University by Madan Mohan Malaviya, Lucknow part.

    • 1917

    Mahatma Gandhi launches the Champaran Satyagraha.

    1919

  • Anti-Rowlatt Satyagraha: M. K Gandhi started campaign against Rowlatt bill and set up Satyagraha Sabha 24th February AD 1919 at Bombay. During this agitation.
  • Jallianwala Bagh tragedy and the great Amritsar massacre.
  • Montague Chelmsford Reform or the Government of India Act, 1919 announced.

    • 1920

  • First meeting of All Indian Trade Union Congress (AITUC) held at Bombay presided over by Lala Lajpat Rai.
  • Indian National Congress (INC) adopts the Non-cooperation resolution

    • 1921

    Permanent Advisory Council of Princes inaugurated; Council of State and Legislative Assembly inaugurated.

    1922

  • Chauri Chauri incident which led to the suspension of the Non-cooperation movement.
  • Vishwa Bharati University started by Rabindranath Tagore.

    • 1923

    Swarajist party founded by Motilal Nehru and others.

    1927

    Appointment of the Simon Commission.

    1929

  • Lord Irwin’s announcement that the goal of British policy in India was the grant of domination status.
  • Lahore session of the Congress under Jawaharlal Nehru adopts the goal of complete independence (Poorna Swaraj) for India.

    • 1930

  • Jawaharlal Nehru hoists the tricolour of India on the banks of Ravi in Lahore. First Independence Day observed.
  • Mahatma Gandhi launches the Civil Disobedience Movement with his epic Dandi march.
  • Working committee of INC meets at Sabarmati and passed the Civil Disobediences Movement with his epic Dandi march.

    • 1931

    Gandhi Irwin pact signed. Civil Disobedience movement suspended.

    1932

  • British Prime Minister Ramsay Mac Donald announces the Communal Awards giving separate electorate to Harijans get reserved seats in place of separate electorate.
  • Poona pact signed by which the Harijans get reserved seats in place of separate electorate.

    • 1935

    Government of India Act passed.

    1938

    Haripuran session of the Indian National Congress. Subhash Chandra Bose elected the congress president.

    1939

  • Subhas Chandra Bose resigns the presidentship of the Indian National Congress
  • WORLD WAR-2 started.
  • Muslim League observes the resignation of the congress ministries as Deliverance day.

    • 1940

  • Lahore session of the Muslim league passes the Pakistan Resolution.
  • Congress launches Individual Satyagraha movement.

    • 1942

  • The Cripps mission's proposals are rejected by Congress.
  • Quit India resolution was passed by the Bombay session of the AICC, which led to the start of a historical civil disobedience movement across India.
  • Indian National Army was an armed force formed by Indian nationalists (Mohan Singh) in Southeast Asia during World War II.

    • 1943

  • Subhas Chandra Bose tales over the leadership of Indian National Congress and proclaims formation of the ‘Provisional Government of free India’ at Singapore.
  • Karachi session of the Muslim League adopts the slogan of ‘Divide and Quit’.

    • 1946

  • Royal Air Force Mutiny of 1946 of British and Indian air force units.
  • Constituent Assembly for India meets for the first time.
  • British Prime Minister Attlee announces the Cabinet Mission.

    • 1947

  • British Prime Minister Attlee declares that the British government would leave India by June 1948.
  • Indian Independence Bill introduced in the House of Commons and passed by the British parliament on July 18, 1947.
  • War breaks out between India and Pakistan Administered Kashmir forces in Kashmir.
  • Jawaharlal Nehru becomes the first Prime Minister of India and unfurls the Indian tricolour on the ramparts of the Red Fort, symbolically marking the end of British colonial rule.
  • Finally, INDIANS GOT INDEPENDENCE

    • After INDEPENDENCE...

    How is India after Independence


    What are the changes in its development..

    1948

    Nathuram Godse, a right-wing advocate of Hindu nationalism, assassinates Mahatma Gandhi in New Delhi on January 30.

    1950

    The Constitution was adopted by the Indian Constituent Assembly on 26 November 1949, and came into effect on 26 January 1950 with a democratic government system

    1951

    The first Indian Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru presented the First Five-Year Plan to the Parliament of India.

    1962

  • The Sino-Indian War, also known as the Sino-Indian Border Conflict, occurred between China and India.
  • The state Nagaland is formed.

    • 1965

    The Indo-Pakistani War of 1965 was a culmination of skirmishes that took place between April 1965 and September 1965 between Pakistan and India. Both the countries suffered heavy casualties.

    1966

  • The state of Punjab is divided into three other states – Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, and Punjab.
  • Lal Bahadur Shastri and Pakistani President Ayub Khan meet in Taskent along with the Soviet Union Prime Minister Kosygin, and signs ‘Taskent Agreement’.

    • 1969

    The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is founded.

    1972

  • The state of Meghalaya is formed, under the North-Eastern Areas (Re-organisation) Act, 1971.
  • The Simla Agreement is signed between India and Pakistan on July 2 in Simla, Himachal Pradesh.

    • 1974

    India conducts nuclear tests at Pokhran.

    1975

    The Congress government imposes ‘Emergency’.

    1977

    Emergency ends. The first non-Congress government elected at the Centre.

    1980

    The BJP is formed after internal differences in the Janata Party result in the collapse of the Janata government in 1979.

    1984

  • Operation Blue Star is launched to drive out Sikh extremist religious leader Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale and his followers from the Golden Temple, Amritsar.
  • On December 2 and 3, the Bhopal disaster, also referred as the Bhopal gas tragedy took place at the Union Carbide India Limited pesticide plant in Bhopal,Madhya Pradesh. The government of Madhya Pradesh confirmed a total of 3,787 deaths related to the incident.

    • 1985

    On June 23, Air India Flight 182 was destroyed with a bomb, killing 268 Canadian citizens, 27 British citizens, and 24 Indian citizens. Canadian law enforcement determined that the main suspects in the bombing were members of the Sikh militant group Babbar Khalsa. The attack was said to be a retaliation against India for Operation Blue Star.

    1987

    The state of Goa is formed.

    1990

    Cauvery tribunal is formed to resolve the water dispute between Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

    1992

    The Babri Masjid in demolished. The mosque is believed to be built on a piece on the birthplace of Lord Rama – Ram Janmabhoomi, sparking nationwide communal riots.

    1993

    Series of bomb blasts disturbs the city of Mumbai, killing over 250 people and injuring 700.

    1999

    The Kargil War, also known as Kargil conflict, took place between May and July in the Kargil district of Kashmir and other areas along the Line of Control (LoC).

    2000

    The states of Uttarakhand, Chhattisgarh, and Jharkhand are formed.

    2001

    Five terrorists of Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT) and Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) attacked the Parliament of India on December 13.

    2002

    Communal violence breaks out in Gujarat. A coach of Sabarmati Express is set on fire at Godhra railway station.

    2007

    Pratibha Patil becomes the first woman to be elected President of India.

    2008

    10 members of the Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT) carry a series of coordinated bomb attacks and shooting at prime locations including Taj Hotel, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT) and Leopold Café.

    2011

  • After 28 years, The Indian Cricket team wins the World Cup.
  • Social activist Anna Hazare stages a 12-day hunger strike in Delhi in protest at state corruption.

    • 2013

    On June 16, Uttarakhand witnessed devastating floods and landslides killing over 6,000 people and leaving thousands homeless.

    2014

  • Ending the Nehru-Gandhi dynasty, Narendra Modi becomes the 15th prime minister.
  • India’s 29th state Telangana is formed.

    • 2015

    India and Bangladesh sign a historic territory swap deal allowing residents to choose where to live.

    2016

    The Modi-led government announced demonetisation of Rs 500 and Rs 1,000 notes.

    2017

  • The Goods and Services Tax (GST) introduced on July 1.
  • India creates history by launching 104 satellites by ISRO’s vehicle the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV).
  • Punjab government announces free education to girls from nursery to Ph.D. in all government institutions.

    • 2018

    The Bharatiya Janata Party-led government faces the no-confidence motion brought against it by the Congress-led Opposition. After a 12-hour marathon debate, the BJP defeats the motion.

    2019

  • Pulwama attack: A convoy of vehicles carrying security personnel on the Jammu-Srinagar National Highway was attacked by a suicide bomber near Pulwama District, Jammu and Kashmir.
  • ISRO launches India’s second mission to moon Chandrayaan-2.
  • In the month of December, COVID-19 slowly started to spread in India.

    • 2020

  • Prime Minister Modi announced a 21-day lockdown throughout India from midnight of 25 March to 14 April to prevent the spread of COVID-19.
  • Even Lord SRI VENKATESWARA SWAMY temple, Tirupati has been closed for a few days which has never been done before.
  • Air India Express Flight 1344 Crashed after overrunning the Runway at Calicut International Airport, Kerala. At least 19 people killed, including two pilots and 17 Passengers.

    • 2021

  • Two COVID-19 vaccines-- Covaxin and Covishield-- were approved by India for emergency use.
  • Oxygen Crisis during 2nd wave of COVID pandemic almost everywhere.
  • Three controversial farm laws scrapped and farmers withdraw year-long protest.

    • 2022

  • Karnataka hijab row The high court's interim order was implemented in all schools and colleges across Karnataka, with students, and in some cases teachers, being asked to remove hijabs and burqas outside the school gates.
  • India's first private train under the Bharat Gaurav scheme is launched at Southern Railway zone between Coimbatore and Shirdi. This train is operated by South Star Rail based in Tamil Nadu.
  • On 25 July, Droupadi Murmu is sworn in as the 15th President of India.
